## How opportunities are created

**Inbound — SDR qualified.** Prospect expressed interest via inbound channel. SDR qualifies (BANT), creates opportunity at Stage 1, introduces AE. Qualification notes are the AE's first intelligence asset.

**Outbound — BDR qualified.** BDR prospected into target account and generated interest. BDR creates opportunity, introduces AE, documents qualification conversation. AE inherits a warmer situation but potentially less expressed urgency than inbound.

**Outbound — AE self-sourced.** AE identified and qualified independently. No handoff notes. AE owns full context from first interaction.

## The eight stages

*(Stage names pending final confirmation of exact Salesforce wording.)*

- **Stage 1 — Identifying an Opportunity:** Initial interest established. Opportunity created. Qualification in progress or complete.
- **Stage 2 — Determining Problem, Impact, Ideal:** AE in active discovery. Problem defined, business impact quantified, ideal outcome articulated in customer's language.
- **Stage 3 — Identifying Decision Makers:** Power map being built. Economic buyer, champion, technical validators, potential blockers identified and engaged.
- **Stage 4 — Demonstrating Value:** Tailored demo, POV, or POC delivered or in progress. Value made concrete against the specific problem and stakeholders identified.
- **Stage 5 — Confirming Value with Power:** Economic buyer engaged. Value case presented at budget-control level. Conversation moved to business justification.
- **Stage 6 — Negotiating:** Commercial terms in discussion. Legal, procurement, and security typically active.
- **Stage 7 — Pending:** Agreement reached in principle. Final approvals, signatures, or internal processes in progress.
- **Stage 8 — Closed Won:** Contract executed. Transition to implementation and customer success begins.

## Dead outcomes — code accurately

- **Lost** — customer chose a competing solution. A lost opportunity is not a failed relationship. How you exit determines whether the door stays open.
- **No Decision** — customer completed some or all of the evaluation and chose to do nothing. Usually: internal alignment was never achieved, cost of change outweighed perceived benefit, or the forcing function was resolved by other means. Not permanently disqualified — conditions for a decision did not exist at that time.
- **No Opportunity** — Stage 1 prospect never responded, or AE qualification found no genuine opportunity within a reasonable timeframe. Earliest and least costly exit.

## The handoff — creating continuity

When the SDR or BDR introduces the AE, the quality of the handoff determines how the first AE conversation begins. The language that creates continuity:

*"I can see from the notes in your conversation with [name] that you discussed [specific topic]. Can you tell me a bit more about that?"*

This signals the previous conversation was heard and recorded. Gives confidence that time will not be wasted repeating ground already covered. Allows the AE to enter at depth, not at the surface.

## Cycles and no

Enterprise cycles run three months to two years. Documentation is the mechanism that keeps the opportunity alive through personnel change on both sides.

No is a regular feature of the cycle, not an exit signal. Each no is information: what is missing in the value case, who has not been reached, what internal obstacle has not been surfaced. The response to no is curiosity — what would need to be different? — not pressure or withdrawal.

Get to yes or no fast. Treat a slow maybe as the outcome to avoid above all others. A no at Stage 2 costs a week. A no at Stage 6 costs six months.

## Sales cycle rules for JaysAgent

- Always reference prior qualification notes when opening an AE-level conversation. Name the person the customer spoke to previously and what was discussed.
- BANT is the SDR/BDR qualification lens. The MSP takes over once the AE is engaged.
- Every dead opportunity must be coded correctly. The reason matters for re-engagement timing and forecast integrity.
- Stage progression reflects real advancement, not activity volume.
- No is not a disqualifier. It is data. Respond with curiosity, not pressure or withdrawal.
