## Qualification is continuous

The CRM picture is never the full picture. Qualification is not a gate at the start — it is a continuous read on whether the conditions for success exist and whether they are improving or deteriorating.

**Primary disqualification filters:**
- A CTO or senior stakeholder who refuses to meet with vendors (deeply political, past vendor damage, or decisions made elsewhere)
- A partner working their own agenda rather than collaboratively
- A project of necessity, not belief — mandated migrations close but do not expand
- A champion with no internal credibility or authority
- A procurement process that has been running too long with no movement (sponsor has lost support or budget redirected)
- Unspoken follow-through failures — one instance is data, a pattern is a signal to reassess

**Willingness to solve a problem is the first filter — always.** Not seniority, not confirmed budget, not org chart position.

## How to exit without burning the relationship

*"It sounds like you might not be ready for this yet."*

Or, where genuine potential exists but conditions are wrong:
*"Your time is very valuable and it feels like we're attempting to push a boulder up a hill. I have no issue doing that — but without reinforcements, this will require an unwavering commitment from both of us. Are you in a position to dedicate the time required to bring others onboard? Is that achievable?"*

A bad-fit deal today is often a right-fit deal in 12 months. Leaving with honesty and respect keeps the door open.

## Political accounts — two plays

**Play 1 — Find the next necessary project.** Every organisation has mandated work coming. Read public executive commentary, quarterly financial reports, press releases, investor announcements. Find former users of your products inside the account. Build a POV from the inside out that cuts through the politics.

**Play 2 — Find someone senior and make them a hero.** Find the person above or beside the blocker who has budget authority and appetite to be seen driving a strategic initiative. Bring them intelligence they do not have. Give them a win they can present upward. When they move, the blocker moves with them.

## BANT — the Stage 1 threshold

Applied by SDR/BDR before passing to AE. Not a rigid checklist — a minimum viability assessment.

- *Budget* — does the organisation have the financial means? Confirmed number not required. Just enough confidence that budget is not an automatic disqualifier.
- *Authority* — has the conversation reached someone with meaningful purchase influence, or is there a clear path to that person?
- *Need* — is there a problem worth solving? Has the prospect acknowledged a challenge, gap, or aspiration the solution could address?
- *Timing* — is there a reason to act in a reasonable timeframe, or an indefinite exploration with no forcing function?

BANT answers one question: is this worth an AE's time? Once the AE takes over, the MSP deepens these dimensions rather than checking them off.

## Qualification rules for JaysAgent

- Qualify continuously, not just at Stage 1.
- Willingness to solve a problem is the first and primary filter.
- A champion who cannot open doors upward is not yet a champion — appreciate them, find someone senior.
- One follow-through failure is data. A pattern is a disqualification signal.
- Political accounts require a different play, not disqualification — find the necessary project or find the hero.
- Qualifying out is honesty, not failure. How you exit determines whether the door stays open.
- Qualification is investment management. Every hour on an account that will not close is an hour not spent on one that will.
