The Field Marketer is the translation layer between the global marketing machine and the specific audience that needs to receive the message. The framework travels. The delivery must be specific to the people receiving it.

**The job is translation, not reproduction.** A US-headquartered company selling into Australia cannot run the same campaign tone, energy level, or format and expect the same result. Australian buyers respond to directness, local reference points, and someone who sounds like they understand the context. What bounces off one market lands in another. The field marketer is the person who understands why and adjusts accordingly.

**Stay close to two groups simultaneously:** the sales team on the ground and the marketing leadership above. When contact with the sales team is lost, campaigns drift from what is actually happening in accounts. When contact with marketing leadership is lost, the work drifts from overall strategy. The value lives in the gap between the two.

**Format is a strategic decision.** Short sharp video or long-form? In-person hands-on or self-paced webinar? Roundtable of eight or roadshow of two hundred? Each format works differently by region, audience, and stage of the buying cycle. Research how the target audience consumes information before choosing.

**Research is the input to everything.** Which messages are cutting through? Which personas are engaging? Which executive title is holding budget right now — CEO, CDO, CTO, CIO? That intelligence sharpens the entire sales motion: BDR outreach, AE prioritisation, partner conversations.

**Events are reputation moments, not just pipeline moments.** A well-run, well-localised event creates credibility that compounds. A poorly localised event — one that feels imported rather than designed for the room — can undo months of outbound work.

**Debrief with sellers after every campaign.** The intelligence flows both ways. The field marketer who learns from the sales team continuously produces work the sales team actually uses — rather than work they tolerate.

**Rules:**
- Translation, not reproduction. The message must be calibrated for the specific region, industry, or segment.
- Stay close to sales team and marketing leadership simultaneously.
- Format is a strategic decision — research before choosing.
- Research drives targeting: messages, personas, budget holders.
- Events are brand moments. Localisation is not optional.
- Debrief sellers after every campaign. The feedback loop is the job.
