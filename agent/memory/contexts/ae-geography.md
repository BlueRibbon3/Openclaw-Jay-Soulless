The Geography-Based AE specialises in use cases, not industries. Front-end marketing motion, middle-office operations, back-end data infrastructure — these cut across every vertical. Build depth here and apply it horizontally.

**Use cases over industries.** Personalisation exists in retail, FSI, healthcare, technology. Unified platform for IT scalability exists everywhere. Zero-copy data access is an infrastructure problem that belongs to no single industry. When you can map where the pain lives in the business structure — front end, middle, back end — and connect it to a use case you have seen solved before, that is your version of industry knowledge.

**Curiosity is more important here than for a specialist.** The Industry Specialist AE can make assumptions. The Geography-Based AE cannot. Every assumption that has not been confirmed by the prospect is a liability. Ask more, not less, even when confidence is high.

**Do not deliver a detailed POV before discovery has earned it.** A provocative statement or peer reference to open is appropriate. Specificity before the picture is clear is not — regardless of how familiar the use case feels.

**The internal network is the specialist layer.** After discovery, turn inward. Who has solved this before? What did they learn? What would they tell someone approaching a similar account? Other AEs in the region and globally are resources, not competitors. The AE who debriefs with a colleague after discovery and walks into the next conversation with that perspective is operating above the AE who works alone.

**Partners and industry experts are force multipliers.** A partner with vertical depth, or an internal industry expert, adds the layer the Geography AE cannot provide alone. Bring them in early. Brief them well enough to add value immediately.

**Qualification must be harder here than anywhere.** The breadth of the market is a pipeline risk. Disqualify fast. Concentrate time on winnable opportunities. Spreading across twenty interesting conversations instead of eight real ones is the most common failure mode.

**Each conversation starts fresh.** The residue of the previous industry does not travel. Show up genuinely present in each prospect's world.

**Rules:**
- Specialisation is use cases, not industries. Map pain to front end / middle / back end, then to use case.
- Every assumption not confirmed by the prospect is a liability.
- Provocative opening is fine. Detailed POV before discovery has earned it is not.
- After discovery: internal network first, then partners, then internal industry experts.
- Qualification is harder here. Disqualify fast. Protect the calendar ruthlessly.
- Less selling, more understanding — even when the use case feels familiar.
