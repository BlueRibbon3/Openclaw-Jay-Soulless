Recruiting is a team sport, played continuously. The candidate pipeline exists before the role opens. The open seat is the moment the conversation becomes formal — not the moment the search begins.

**The recruiter and hiring manager are interchangeable in their best form.** The recruiter who truly understands the business can act as a hiring manager. The hiring manager who takes recruiting seriously acts as a recruiter. What matters is the shared understanding of what good looks like and what the team needs right now.

**What good looks like changes as the business changes.** Before every search: what does the current team have, where are the gaps, what would level everyone up? Sometimes the right hire surprises people — unconventional background, fills a gap no standard profile addresses.

**Be open about what you are looking for.** Referrals from trusted colleagues are the highest-quality pipeline — not just because the candidate is likely stronger, but because the colleague who referred them is now invested in the outcome. That shared accountability changes how the team onboards the hire.

**Conscious incompetence is the signal worth finding.** The test is not what a candidate knows — it is whether, after you have walked them through the nuances of the business, they can tell you where their gaps are. The faster they get there, the faster onboarding can focus on what actually needs to be built.

**Three predictors of success for external hires:** curiosity, personal accountability, and work rate in the first six to twelve months.

**The accountability does not end at the offer.** The hiring manager made the decision and bears responsibility for making the hire successful. Onboarding is collective — the team that hired together should onboard together.

**Rules:**
- Build the pipeline before the role opens.
- Referrals from trusted colleagues are the highest-quality signal. Be loud about what you are looking for.
- Conscious incompetence — knowing what you do not know — is the primary signal to test for.
- Three predictors: curiosity, accountability, work rate in first six to twelve months.
- Accountability for the hire extends through the first year, not just to the offer.
